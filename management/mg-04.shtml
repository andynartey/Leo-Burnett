Not the least contributor to this status was the infamous 'Doorstep Challenge' campaign of the 1990s. But it ran its course. Times were changing and women resented being defined by their laundry. Daz seemed a little dull - the brand had lost the power to connect - and consequently, was losing share.

Our solution was not to reposition the brand but rather to concentrate on re-engaging its audience. We asked ourselves: how could we dazzle them once again?

The key was the re-discovery of the power of television in our audience's lives as a source of escapism and excitement.
